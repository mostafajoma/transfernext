Features & Benefits

TransferNext uses a mechanism known as a securitized token offering (STO) to help you source funds from your investors. An STO involves the issue of tokens to finance a project through smart contracts, which are blockchain-based computer programs intended to automatically execute, control, or document legally relevant events or actions.

TransferNext’s services are built on the back of Ethereum, the second largest blockchain network in the world with thousands of applications operating on it. Ethereum is a decentralized technology that leverages a “proof of work” (PoW) validation system to confirm all transactions that happen on its networks. PoW is a form of cryptographic zero-knowledge proof in which one party (the prover) proves to others (the verifiers) that a certain amount of computational effort has been expended for some purpose. Verifiers can subsequently confirm this effort with minimal effort on their part.

Traditional fund transfers generally require a multi-day settlement period in order for funds to be moved from one account to another. The need for back office professionals to move funds restricts transfers to business hours, and demands higher costs to pay for this headcount. On a blockchain, the distributed validation of all transactions across the network by computers around the world allow for an immediate transfer of funds at any time you or one of your investors choose to execute. Integration with wire transfer services via SWIFT protocol will allow for funds to be moved directly in or out of bank accounts to streamline the conversion of cash to tokens on both sides of the transaction.

An advantage of using tokens to represent a given amount of investment in a fund is that they can be transferred between investors (given that they meet the legal requirements to invest in such funds). This can allow investors to liquidate their position in a fund without a redemption, ensuring that the amount of funds held remains constant over the investment horizon.

For fund managers who seek to raise capital from a variety of investors who may be liquid in funds other than their base currency, the use of Ether as an intermediary currency for transactions can be used to quickly source funds from foreign investors without the need for potentially costly large-scale foreign exchange conversions from one currency to another. Ether is the cryptocurrency of the Ethereum blockchain network, and can be bought with most traditional sovereign currencies. If desired, Ether can be used in order to facilitate the purchase of your fund’s tokens, which can then be converted into your base currency for investments.
